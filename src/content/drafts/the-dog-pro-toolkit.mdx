As part of my exploration for an Antropian product (an in-house product basically) I interviewed dog professionals: a shop owner, a photographer and an educator. Three very different jobs, but all orbiting the same world.

The most interesting pattern is that these people have spent years refining their workflows, one step at a time. They've searched, trialed, and settled.

**Their setups aren't pretty by engineering standards, but they work.**

That's both the insight and the challenge here. Established professionals are great to learn from, but they are unlikely to switch to something new. If it ain't broke, they won't fix it.

The world is not static though. With each passing day there are more professionals dedicating their time to work with dogs. And they all have to go through the same rite.

Here are some of the patterns I noticed across interviews, and the <SideNote note="As with the other exploratory posts, I'm presenting them here with a marketing tone to them, just for fun">product ideas they point to</SideNote>.

# Annoying scheduling

For some of the professionals I talked with, one of the most time-consuming aspects of their work was coordinating sessions with clients. This process usually requires a lot of back-and-forth to get there.

Both sides have constraints and preferences.

A photographer needs to plan trips to other cities for sessions. An educator prefers to group all sessions together so the rest of the week is free for paperwork and focus time. Clients can only do Mondays, but Mondays are full.

You see the problem, right?

**Insights and open questions**

- **Two-sided problem**: It's finding agreement that is hard, or testing if this or that date works for both sides. Not sure if there is an algorithmic solution here.
- **Generic solutions exist**: The interviews mentioned some of them (i.e. Calendly), but they dropped it. The reason is that tools that let clients pick any open slot works against their optimization. They don't take into account personal planning or relocation times. On top of that these solutions are purely digital, videocalls, meetings, etc. They don't account for the physical aspect of a photography session. Finding the differentiator that works for these people is what could make it worth it.
- **Volume matters**: The two professionals I talked with both called it "*an annoyance*", not a breaking point. This might mean that if solved, it'd only work for professionals with enough clients that the back-and-forth eats meaningful time.
- **Human touch tension**: The problem is strictly in finding the slot, nothing else. All professionals explicitly told me that chatting with their clients is part of their service, and therefore of the value they provide. Any solution in this space needs to make very clear this is all about logistics, not stepping into the human connection between them.

## Timelink

![An AI-generated illustration of a woman upside down drowning in invoices](/blog/the-dog-pro-toolkit/timelink.png)

> Your calendar already knows when you're busy. Timelink fills in the gaps with the sessions that make sense, and lets your clients pick from the ones that work for you.

Nobody likes the tedious back-and-forth of scheduling sessions. What if you want to batch all sessions on Monday? Or you need to plan a trip to a different city? Generic calendar services don't account for your business. Every calendar should be unique, personal, and adapt to you.

- **Preferred slots**: Time slots are not equal. You want clients to pick Fridays first because it better fits your work week. Or you want to pack sessions together to make room for focus time. Timelink will nudge clients to follow your preferences without hard restrictions.
- **Event blocks**: A session isn't just the session. There's travel, preparation, introductions, wrap-up. You define your structure once, and Timelink creates all the calendar events for you. Always on time, always prepared.
- **Location-aware events**: Moving to a different city is not the same as having an online session. Timelink add dynamic buffers depending on location.
- **Calendar-aware suggestions**: Connects to your Google or Apple calendar and shows your week as blocks. Quickly create available time blocks for your clients to pick.
- **One link, no account**: Generates a simple link you drop into a WhatsApp conversation. Your client sees only the options you picked, chooses one, done. No app to install, no login, no platform to learn.
- **Per-event buffers**: Add buffer time before and after each session for travel and preparation times.
- **Auto-update**: When a client picks a slot, your calendar updates and the link adjusts the remaining options or deactivates. You get a notification in WhatsApp, not in yet another app.
